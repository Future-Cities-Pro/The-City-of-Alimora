The City Overview

On the southern coast of Brazil, surrounded by lush green mountains, sandy beaches, and turquoise waters, lies the city of Alimora. This remarkable city boasts a zero-waste circular economy and an unparalleled quality of life for its two million citizens.

Food, transportation, and waste management are provided to maintain healthy lifestyles. A variety of careers are available, allowing everyone the opportunity to thrive. During their free time, residents can participate in water activities at Alimora Bay, pick fresh fruits and vegetables in community parks, relax in public gathering areas, and visit the city's arcades.

The Challenge We Faced

Before Alimora, the southern coast of Brazil faced a sustainability crisis

Crops grown on rural farms were transported long distances into cities in massive quantities. The long distances and spoilage created a humanitarian and environmental disaster:

4,800

Water pollution from leachate

Much of this waste was sent to landfills or left on the street, where it sat spoiled and uneaten. Rotten food created leachate, a dark, acidic liquid rich in contaminants that entered waterways, drastically polluting the water and threatening public health.

The Solutions

How Alimora transformed waste into wealth

Solution 1: Vertical Farming Towers

The Hydroponic-Aeroponic Integrated Life Systems (HAILS) represents a quantum leap in food production technology.

Plants are genetically engineered using CRISPR-Cas9 technology to thrive in vertical environments. Perennial cycles are shortened to annual cycles, vines are modified to be compact, and root vegetables are engineered to grow perfectly in aeroponic systems.
